02 Tour Script

The Sun Oaks experience in 8 stops

A guided conversation framework—not a rigid script. Adapt based on what you learn about each prospect.

1

Welcome & Discovery

Front Desk / Lobby
"Welcome to Sun Oaks! I'm [Name]. Before I show you around, I'd love to learn a little about what brought you in today. What are you hoping to get out of a membership?"
Listen for: fitness goals, family needs, schedule constraints, past gym experiences
Take mental notes to reference throughout the tour
2

The Heart of Sun Oaks

Main Fitness Floor
"This is our main fitness floor—about 15,000 square feet. You'll notice it's busy but never crowded. We've designed the layout so you can always find space to work. See that group over there? Many of them have been training together for years. That's the thing about Sun Oaks—people come for the equipment and stay for the community."
Point out specific equipment relevant to their stated goals
Introduce them to a trainer if one is available
3

Group Fitness Studios

Studio A & B
"We run over 120 classes a week across two studios. Everything from yoga and Pilates to high-intensity training and cycling. Most members tell us the classes are what keep them coming back—the instructors know your name, and the regulars become your workout family."
Hand them a class schedule if they express interest
Mention specific classes that match their interests
4

Aquatics Center

Pool Deck
"Three pools, one mission: water for everyone. Lap swimmers in the morning, families in the afternoon, swim lessons year-round. A lot of our members learned to swim here as kids—and now they're teaching their own children in the same pool."
If they have kids, emphasize the learn-to-swim program
Mention water aerobics for low-impact fitness seekers
5

Tennis & Pickleball

Courts
"Ten tennis courts and four dedicated pickleball courts. If you play, you'll never struggle to find a game—we have leagues, clinics, and open play every day. If you don't play yet, our pros will have you serving in no time. Pickleball especially has brought in a whole new community."
Ask about their racquet sport experience
Mention the social aspect of leagues
6

Kids Club

Children's Area
"For families, this is the game-changer. Kids Club is included with family memberships—safe, supervised, and fun. You get your workout in while your kids play. During summer, our camps keep them active all day. Many parents tell us this is the reason they can actually maintain a fitness routine."
Only include this stop if they have children
Emphasize the "workout without guilt" benefit
7

Spa & Recovery

Spa Wing
"Recovery is part of fitness. Our spa offers massage, skincare, and recovery services. Members get preferred pricing, and many build a monthly massage into their wellness routine. It's also a popular spot for birthday gifts and self-care days."
Mention member discounts on services
Quick stop unless they show strong interest
8

The Close

Membership Desk
"So that's Sun Oaks. Based on what you told me about [reference their goals], I think you'd get a lot out of [specific programs]. The [recommended tier] would give you everything you need. What questions can I answer before we get you started?"
Connect back to their original stated goals
Assume the sale—"get you started" not "if you decide"
03 Objection Handling

Turning "no" into "tell me more"

Every objection is a request for more information. Here's how to respond with empathy and confidence.

$ "It's too expensive."

This is rarely about the actual dollar amount—it's about perceived value. Your job is to connect the price to what matters to them specifically.

Try This "I understand—it's an investment. Can I ask what you're comparing it to? ... When you break it down, it's about $3 a day for unlimited access to fitness, pools, courts, classes, and community. Most members tell us they actually save money because they cancel other subscriptions and spend less eating out when they're feeling healthy."
⏱ "I need to think about it."

This usually means there's an unaddressed concern. Gently probe to find out what's really holding them back.

Try This "Of course—this is a decision worth getting right. Just so I can help: is there something specific you're weighing, or something I didn't cover well enough? Sometimes people say they need to think about it when really there's a question I haven't answered yet."
👫 "I need to talk to my spouse."

Respect this completely—but help them have a productive conversation at home by understanding what questions might come up.

Try This "Absolutely—that makes sense for a family decision. What do you think they'll want to know? I can make sure you have everything you need for that conversation. Would it help if I put together a quick summary you can share with them?"
📍 "I'm not sure I'll use it enough."

This fear is real—everyone's joined a gym they didn't use. Address it directly by showing how Sun Oaks is different.

Try This "That's a fair concern—nobody wants to pay for something they don't use. Here's what I've noticed: the members who stick around aren't just coming for equipment. They're coming because they've found their people. Once you're in a class with regulars who notice when you're not there, or you've got a Tuesday tennis game you look forward to, it stops feeling like 'going to the gym' and starts feeling like going to see friends."
🏋️ "I can work out at home."

Don't argue against home workouts—acknowledge them and expand the conversation to what they can't get at home.

Try This "Home workouts are great for consistency—I do them too sometimes. But can I ask: what's missing from your home routine? For most people, it's the variety, the accountability, and honestly, the separation from home life. When you walk into Sun Oaks, you're in fitness mode. No laundry staring at you, no kids interrupting. Plus, you can't swim laps or play pickleball in your living room."
📋 "I don't want a long contract."

Contract concerns are usually about feeling trapped. Clarify your terms and emphasize flexibility.

Try This "I get it—nobody likes feeling locked in. Here's how we work: we have month-to-month options with no long-term commitment required. We do offer a discount for annual commitments if you prefer that, but it's your choice. We'd rather earn your membership every month than hold you to a contract you resent."
04 Closing Framework

Four paths to yes

Different prospects need different approaches. Read the room and choose accordingly.

01 The Assumptive Close

For the ready buyer

When they're clearly excited and engaged throughout the tour, don't overcomplicate it. Move directly to logistics.

"Let's get you set up. I'll need a driver's license and a card for the monthly billing. Which tier makes the most sense for your situation?"
02 The Summary Close

For the analytical buyer

When they've asked lots of questions and want to feel like they're making an informed decision.

"So you're looking for [goals]. You loved the [specific areas]. The [tier] at $X/month gives you all of that plus [additional benefits]. Does that cover everything you need?"
03 The Urgency Close

For the procrastinator

When they like everything but seem content to delay. Create a reason to act today—but only if it's genuine.

"I should mention—we're running [current promotion] through [date]. If you start today, you'll [specific benefit]. After that, we go back to standard rates."
04 The Trial Close

For the skeptic

When they're interested but hesitant. Lower the barrier by offering a way to experience before committing.

"I can tell you're not quite ready to commit—that's okay. How about this: come in for a week on us. Try the classes, use the pools, see how it feels. If it's not for you, no pressure. If it is, we'll apply your trial toward your first month."
