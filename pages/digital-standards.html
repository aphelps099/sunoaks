Chapter Ten

A website should make people want to join.

The difference between a website that informs and a website that converts is simple: one makes you feel something. The other just lists features.

First Impression
Users decide to stay or leave in the first three seconds. Photography and clarity matter more than content.

Inspire first. Inform second.
Photos should make you want to be there.
Copy should get out of the way.

01
Photography

Photos that move people

Great photography doesn't just show facilities—it captures energy, community, and the feeling of being there. Every image should make someone think: "I want to be part of that."

Avoid Generic

Empty facility shots

A photo of empty courts at 2pm with flat lighting. Equipment lined up like a catalog. No people, no energy, no life. This shows what you have—not why anyone should care.

Brand Standard Authentic

Real members, golden hour

Action mid-play. Golden hour light streaming through trees. Real members in motion. This is what 64 years of community actually looks like.

Avoid Staged

Static, staged shots

Perfect symmetry. Nobody moving. Feels like a brochure from 1998. Photos like this create distance—they say nothing about your community.

Brand Standard Energy

Motion and energy

A serve at full extension. Concentration. Athletic grace. This is the feeling we're selling—not a list of amenities.

Real Moments

Facility

Golden Hour

Shoot within 2 hours of sunrise or sunset. The warm Redding light creates depth and emotion that midday can't match.

Real Community

Feature actual members with signed releases. Three generations. All body types. Your real community, not a casting call.

Capture Motion

A splash in the pool. A tennis serve. Mid-spin in cycling class. Motion creates energy. Static poses feel like stock.

Connection

Eye contact. High fives. Conversation between sets. The social fabric that makes Sun Oaks different from a box gym.

Composition

Leave room for text overlay on hero shots. Rule of thirds. Avoid centering—create visual interest with asymmetry.

Technical

Minimum 2000px wide for web. Edit for warmth, not HDR. Consistent color grading across all images.

02
Calls to Action

One action per screen

Every additional button reduces the chance someone clicks any of them. Great websites guide visitors toward one clear action—not a buffet of options.

Current Approach
About Tennis Fitness Aquatics Contact
Become a Member Schedule Tour Learn More
Multiple competing CTAs. "Become a Member" competes with "Schedule Tour" competes with "Learn More." Each dilutes the others. The visitor doesn't know what you want them to do.
Brand Standard
Start Your Free Trial → Or schedule a tour
One primary action. Clear hierarchy. The secondary option is understated but available. The visitor knows exactly what to do next.

Primary vs. Secondary

One bold button per viewport. Secondary actions should be text links, not competing buttons.

Action Language

"Start Your Free Trial" beats "Learn More." Verbs that describe the outcome, not the process.

Sun Gold CTAs

Primary buttons use Sun Yellow (#F7B500) with black text. Nothing else should be this color.

Reduce Friction

Every form field reduces completion. Ask only what you need. Make it easy.

03
Layout

Layouts that breathe

White space isn't empty space—it's breathing room. Cluttered layouts overwhelm. Clean layouts guide the eye and build trust.

01

Hero First

The hero should be 80-100% of the viewport. One powerful image. One clear headline. One call to action. Nothing else.

02

Progressive Disclosure

Don't dump everything on the homepage. Lead with emotion, reveal details as they scroll. Let curiosity pull them deeper.

03

Mobile-First

60%+ of visitors are on phones. Design for the small screen first. What works there will work everywhere.

04
Copy

Less copy, more impact

Nobody reads websites—they scan them. Dense paragraphs get skipped. Short, punchy copy gets read. Every word should earn its place.

Current Approach
Sun Oaks Tennis & Fitness has been Redding, California's finest full-service health club for more than 60 years. Located on over six beautiful acres in east Redding, we offer 7 outdoor and 3 indoor tennis courts—the only indoor courts in the North State—plus 4 dedicated pickleball courts. Our facility features three pools and two spas for your enjoyment. Our covered 8-lane, 25-yard pool is open and heated year round. We also offer 120+ group fitness classes weekly...
Brand Standard
For sixty-four years, Sun Oaks has been the place.

Three generations of families. Morning swimmers and after-school tennis kids.

This is where Redding grows—together.
01

Lead with Why

Don't open with features. Open with meaning. Why should someone care? What feeling are you selling?

02

Cut by Half

Write your copy. Then delete half. Then half again. What remains is usually stronger.

03

Scannable Structure

Headlines that tell the story. Short paragraphs. Bullet points for features. People scan before they read.

The Opportunity

A website that matches who Sun Oaks actually is.

Sun Oaks has 64 years of legacy. Three generations of families. The only indoor courts in the North State. A community that actually shows up for each other. The digital presence should reflect that.
